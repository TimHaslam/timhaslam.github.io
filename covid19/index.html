Project Overview

The spread of the SARS-CoV-2 virus and its associated syndrome, Coronavirus Disease 2019 (COVID-19), has resulted in a pressing need to screen individuals and populations. We are performing a head-to-head comparison of commercially available immunoassays (e.g. immunochromatographic strip tests (ICT) and enzyme immunoassays (EIA)), including an evaluation of performance by time from symptom onset. Our goal is to provide comprehensive real-world test performance data to inform the scientific and medical community, government organizations, and the general public.

Test Design Characteristics

Our goal is to provide comprehensive real-world test performance data to inform the scientific and medical community, government organizations, and the general public.

Sample Selection

Remnant serum and plasma samples from routine laboratory testing are analyzed from patients with concurrent SARS-CoV-2 qPCR testing and clinical data. Pre-2019 historical blood donor serum serves as additional negative control.

Immunoassay Testing

ICT and EIA testing is performed at UCSF. Plate-based EIA testing is performed in duplicate using standardized plate washers and readers. ICT testing is performed by >2 readers blinded to test result. Assays are assigned a qualitative result based off of package insert-derived cutoffs. Quantitative results are derived from optical density for EIA and reader assigned integer score (0-6) for ICT band intensity.

Data Analysis

Clinical sensitivity and specificity are calculated using SAR-CoV-2 qPCR results (any positive result for the patient counted as confirmed infection) as the comparator. Sensitivity and specificity data are also assessed for different intervals from symptom onset (0-5d, 6-10d, 11-15d, >16d).
